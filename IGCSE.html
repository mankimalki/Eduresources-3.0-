The Complete IGCSE Guide: Syllabus, Resources & Exam Tips

Last Updated: January 15, 2025 10 min read By EduResources Team

The International General Certificate of Secondary Education (IGCSE) is one of the most popular international qualifications for 14 to 16-year-olds worldwide. In this comprehensive guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about IGCSE exams - from syllabus details and subject choices to study resources and exam strategies that will help you achieve top grades.

What is IGCSE?

The IGCSE is an internationally recognized qualification developed by Cambridge Assessment International Education (CAIE). It's equivalent to the GCSE in the UK and is accepted by universities and employers worldwide.

Key Fact: Over 10,000 schools in 160 countries offer Cambridge IGCSE, making it the world's most popular international qualification for 14-16 year olds.

IGCSE vs GCSE: Key Differences

Feature IGCSE GCSE
Curriculum International focus UK-focused
Grading A*-G or 9-1 9-1 (England)

IGCSE Subject Choices

Cambridge offers over 70 subjects at IGCSE level, including 30 languages. Students typically take 7-10 subjects, with a mix of:

  • Core subjects: English, Mathematics, Sciences
  • Additional subjects: Humanities, Languages, Arts, Technology
  • Electives: Business Studies, Computer Science, etc.

IGCSE Grading System

Cambridge IGCSE uses two grading scales:

  • A*-G scale: A* is the highest grade, G is the lowest passing grade
  • 9-1 scale: 9 is the highest grade, 1 is the lowest (similar to GCSE)

Most universities and employers consider grades A*-C (or 9-4) as passes. Some competitive courses may require A*/A (9-7) grades in relevant subjects.

Pro Tip: Many universities consider IGCSE grades when making offers, especially for competitive courses like Medicine or Law.

IGCSE Exam Preparation Tips

Follow these expert strategies to maximize your IGCSE results:

1. Understand the Syllabus

Download the syllabus for each subject and use it as a checklist. Mark topics as you cover them to ensure you don't miss anything.

2. Create a Study Schedule

Plan your revision in advance, allocating more time to challenging subjects. The 80/20 rule suggests focusing on the 20% of material that appears in 80% of exams.

3. Practice with Past Papers

Complete past papers under timed conditions to simulate the exam experience. Analyze your mistakes and focus on weak areas.

4. Use Active Recall

Instead of passive reading, test yourself regularly. Create flashcards or teach concepts to someone else to reinforce learning.

5. Master Exam Technique

Learn command words (e.g., "describe", "explain", "evaluate") and how to structure different question types for maximum marks.

Time Management Tip: In exams, allocate time based on marks - spend about 1 minute per mark. Leave time at the end to check your work.

IGCSE Frequently Asked Questions

When are IGCSE exams held?

Cambridge IGCSE exams are held twice a year - in May/June and October/November. Some subjects also have a March series in certain countries.

How long does it take to get IGCSE results?

Results for May/June exams are typically released in mid-August, while October/November results come out in mid-January.

Can I retake IGCSE exams?

Yes, you can retake IGCSE exams in any subsequent exam series. There's no limit to the number of times you can retake.

Do universities accept IGCSE?

Yes, IGCSE is widely recognized by universities worldwide, including the UK, US, Canada, Australia, and Europe.
