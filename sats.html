The Complete Parent's Guide to SATs in the UK

Everything you need to know about Key Stage 1 and 2 assessments

Last updated: June 2025 8 min read By EduResources Team

SATs (Standard Assessment Tests) are national curriculum assessments taken by primary school children in England. They're designed to evaluate your child's progress and the school's performance. If you're a parent of a Year 2 or Year 6 student, this comprehensive guide will help you understand everything about SATs, from what they are to how you can best support your child.

What Are SATs?

SATs are standardized tests taken by children in English primary schools at the end of Key Stage 1 (Year 2, age 6-7) and Key Stage 2 (Year 6, age 10-11). They assess children's knowledge of the national curriculum in:

  • English (reading, grammar, punctuation and spelling)
  • Mathematics
  • Science (for some Year 6 children)

Key Point: SATs results help secondary schools understand each child's academic level and identify areas where they may need additional support.

SATs Timetable 2025

Key Stage 1 SATs (Year 2)

These assessments take place throughout May 2025. Schools have flexibility in administration:

  • Reading: Two papers (combined time approx. 40 minutes)
  • Mathematics: Two papers (arithmetic and reasoning)
  • English grammar, punctuation and spelling: Optional paper

Key Stage 2 SATs (Year 6)

These take place in the same week nationally (12-16 May 2025):

  • Monday: English grammar, punctuation and spelling (45 mins + 15 mins spelling)
  • Tuesday: English reading (60 mins)
  • Wednesday: Mathematics Paper 1 (arithmetic, 30 mins) and Paper 2 (reasoning, 40 mins)
  • Thursday: Mathematics Paper 3 (reasoning, 40 mins)

How Are SATs Marked?

SATs results are reported using scaled scores:

  • 100: Expected standard
  • Below 100: Working towards expected standard
  • Above 100: Working at greater depth

The exact raw score needed to achieve 100 varies each year based on the difficulty of the tests.

Key Stage 1 Assessments

These are marked by teachers within the school, with some external moderation. Results are reported to parents and used to inform teacher assessments.

Key Stage 2 Tests

These are marked externally, with results sent to schools in July. Parents receive their child's results before the end of the summer term.

SATs FAQ

Are SATs compulsory?

While schools are required to administer the tests, parents can withdraw their children from SATs. However, this is generally not recommended as the tests provide valuable information about your child's progress.

How important are SATs results?

SATs results help secondary schools set appropriate work, but they don't determine which secondary school your child attends. The Year 6 results may be used to set initial groups in Year 7, but these are usually reviewed after the first term.

What if my child is ill during SATs week?

Schools can arrange for your child to take missed tests shortly afterwards if they're well enough. If they miss all tests, the school will base their assessment on other work.

How can I help my child with test anxiety?

Keep things in perspective, maintain routines, ensure good sleep, and focus on effort rather than results. Practice relaxation techniques like deep breathing together.
