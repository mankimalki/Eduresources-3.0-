The Complete 11 Plus Exam Guide for 2025

Everything you need to know to help your child succeed in grammar school entrance tests

Last updated: January 2025 Reading time: 8 minutes By EduResources Team

The 11 Plus exam is a crucial milestone for many UK students aiming to enter grammar schools. With competition for places often fierce, proper preparation is essential. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about the 11 Plus in 2025, including exam structure, preparation strategies, and free resources to help your child succeed.

What is the 11 Plus Exam?

The 11 Plus (also called the 11+ or Eleven Plus) is an entrance examination taken by students in England and Northern Ireland in their final year of primary school (Year 6, age 10-11). The exam determines admission to grammar schools and other selective secondary schools.

Key facts about the 11 Plus:

  • Taken by students in Year 6 (typically age 10-11)
  • Used for selective school admissions in 36 local authorities
  • Exam dates vary by region (usually September-January)
  • Results determine grammar school eligibility
  • Format and content varies by region and school

11 Plus Exam Structure

The 11 Plus typically assesses four main subject areas, though the exact combination varies by region:

1. Verbal Reasoning

Tests ability to understand and reason using words, including:

  • Synonyms and antonyms
  • Word analogies
  • Letter and number codes
  • Logical word problems

2. Non-Verbal Reasoning

Assesses problem-solving using visual information:

  • Shape sequences and analogies
  • Pattern recognition
  • Rotation and reflection
  • Hidden shapes

3. Mathematics

Covers Key Stage 2 curriculum with challenging problems:

  • Arithmetic (fractions, percentages, decimals)
  • Word problems
  • Algebraic concepts
  • Geometry and measurement

4. English

Evaluates reading comprehension and writing skills:

  • Reading passages with questions
  • Grammar and punctuation
  • Vocabulary
  • Creative writing (in some regions)

Pro Tip

Check with your target schools or local authority for their specific exam format. Some areas use CEM (Durham University) or GL Assessment papers, which have different styles and content emphasis.

11 Plus Preparation Timeline

Effective preparation should begin well before the exam date. Here's a suggested timeline:

Year 4 (Age 8-9)

  • Develop strong reading habits
  • Practice mental maths daily
  • Introduce puzzle games to build reasoning skills

Year 5 (Age 9-10)

  • Begin formal 11 Plus preparation
  • Identify weak areas through diagnostic tests
  • Establish regular study routine (30-60 minutes daily)
  • Take first practice papers under timed conditions

Year 6 (Age 10-11)

  • Intensive practice (summer before exam)
  • Focus on exam technique and time management
  • Complete full mock exams regularly
  • Review mistakes and refine strategies

Top 11 Plus Preparation Tips

1. Start Early, Progress Gradually

Begin with foundational skills in Year 4, gradually increasing intensity. Cramming rarely works for the 11 Plus.

2. Focus on Weak Areas First

Identify and prioritize your child's weakest subjects early in the preparation process.

3. Develop Exam Technique

Practice time management, question skipping strategies, and multiple-choice skills.

4. Build Vocabulary Systematically

Learn 5-10 new words daily with definitions, synonyms, and usage examples.

5. Simulate Exam Conditions

Regularly complete full practice papers under timed conditions to build stamina.

6. Encourage Independent Thinking

Guide your child to solve problems rather than providing answers directly.

7. Maintain Balance

Ensure your child has time for play, hobbies, and relaxation to prevent burnout.

Wellbeing Tip

Praise effort rather than results. The 11 Plus journey should build confidence and resilience, not just academic skills.

11 Plus FAQs

When should my child start preparing for the 11 Plus?
Most students begin formal preparation in Year 5, but building foundational skills (especially in reading and maths) should start earlier. Intensive preparation typically begins 12-18 months before the exam.
How many hours per week should my child study?
In Year 5, aim for 3-5 hours per week, increasing to 5-8 hours in the final six months. Quality matters more than quantity - focused, regular practice is most effective.
What's the pass mark for the 11 Plus?
There's no universal pass mark. Each school sets its own qualifying score based on competition. Scores are typically standardized and range between 70-140, with most grammar schools requiring 110+.
Can my child take the 11 Plus more than once?
Generally no - the exam is taken once in Year 6. Some areas offer appeals or review procedures for exceptional circumstances.
What if my child doesn't pass?
Many excellent comprehensive schools exist, and students can thrive in any school environment. Some areas offer 12+ or 13+ entry tests for late developers.

Final Thoughts

While the 11 Plus is challenging, with the right preparation and mindset, many students succeed in gaining grammar school places. Remember that the process should be about developing your child's academic abilities and confidence, not just passing an exam.

Use our free resources to support your child's preparation, maintain a balanced approach, and focus on long-term learning rather than short-term results. With consistent effort and the right strategies, your child can perform to their full potential on test day.
